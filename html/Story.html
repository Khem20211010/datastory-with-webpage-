   Hong Kong population


The Hong Kong Resident Population in 2016 was 7,336,585. It had more than doubled during the past 55 years (from 3.17 million in 1961). After rapid growth in the 1960s and 1970s, the population rate of growth slowed down somewhat, to a yearly average of 0.7% for the period 2011-2016.

According to the statistics released by the Census and Statistics Department in August this year, the provisional estimate of the Hong Kong population is 7,394,700 at mid-2021, representing an increase of 0.8% compared to 2016.



   Hong Kong population over the last 60 years


The proportion of population born outside of Hong Kong has been increasing steadily. The number of people from mainland China, Macao or Taiwan was 2.27 million, equivalent to 31%. People born elsewhere accounted for 0.61 million - i.e. 8.4% of Hong Kong's population, also due to the large number of foreign domestic helpers.

In fact, among the non-Chinese, the largest ethnic groups in Hong Kong were Filipinos and Indonesians, constituting 31.5% and 26.2% of the non-Chinese population respectively.

    Finding 1



Majority of domestic helpers

From the latest data, the largest ethnic group in Hong Kong is the Filipinos, which is more than 184 thousand (32%). The group of Indonesians is the secondmost part, which about 153 thousand (26%). The majority of the Filipinos(51%) and Indonesians(45%) work as domestic helpers for Hong Kong family, providing housekeeping, cooking, childcare services.

    Finding 2



Social demographics

The social demographics of the various ethnic minority groups are quite interesting and reflect how different ethnic groups live in Hong Kong.

Although the 2 most represented ethnic groups are Indonesians and Filipinos, most of them are here as Domestic Helpers. In fact, it would not be an understatement to say that almost all domestic helpers are either Indonesian or Filipino.

As a result, one does not usually notice the sheer amount of certain ethnic groups in Hong Kong. For example, I had many Pakistani and Indian classmates during highschool and naturally thought that Indians and Pakistanis covered the bulk of ethnic minorities.

Lastly, some ethnic groups are not included in the pie chart because most of the poeple in those groups are usually well educated and skilled. Therefore, they are most likely foreign workers for their comapnies. For example, most Japanese people in Hong Kong are usually foreign workers for their Japanese companies.

    Finding 3



Income

As we have seen, Hong Kong is indeed a culturally diverse society with people of many different ethnic backgrounds. They are all welcome here, however they appear to fare differently, as a thematic report published by the Census and Statistics Department of the Hong Kong Government in 2018 showed.

White households earned the most among all ethnic minorities, with a median monhtly income of around HKD60,000. They were followed by Koreans, at HKD48,250 and Japanese at HKD40,000.

The average household monthly income for all ethnic minorities was HKD32,500, which notably was higher than Hong Kong's overall figure, that stood at HKD25,000.

In contrast, other Asian households earned less. The median monthly income for Indonesian households in 2016 was given as HKD9,250. In fact, in a separate report released by the Legislative Council that year, it was highlighted that South Asians accounted for almost 50% of those considered to be poor among ethnic minorities.
